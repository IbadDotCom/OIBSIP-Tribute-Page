The Richest Poor Man - Abdul Sattar Edhi

About Abdul Sattar Edhi

Abdul Sattar Edhi (1 January 1928 – 8 July 2016) was a Pakistani philanthropist, social activist, and humanitarian. He was the founder of the Edhi Foundation, which runs hospitals, homeless shelters, schools, and animal shelters across Pakistan. Known as the "Angel of Mercy" and "Pakistan's Father Teresa," Edhi dedicated his life to serving humanity without any discrimination based on race, religion, or nationality.

What I Adore About Abdul Sattar Edhi

What truly inspires me about Abdul Sattar Edhi is his unwavering commitment to helping others selflessly. He lived a simple life, using his resources to build a foundation that provides medical aid, food, and shelter to millions. His humility, compassion, and sense of duty towards humanity are values that the world desperately needs more of. He showed us that one person can indeed make a difference.

"No religion is higher than humanity." – Abdul Sattar Edhi

Legacy

The Edhi Foundation continues to be a beacon of hope for the underprivileged. With hundreds of ambulances, free medical care, and a focus on empowering marginalized communities, the organization ensures Edhi's mission lives on. Abdul Sattar Edhi's work reminds us that empathy and kindness transcend all boundaries.
